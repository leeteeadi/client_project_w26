How to Design the Perfect Portfolio

Why You Need a Portfolio

  • Demonstrate real-world skills: Show concrete examples of your design, research, coding, writing, or analysis work.
  • Tell your story: Walk employers through your process, challenges, and learning outcomes.
  • Stand out from resumes alone: A portfolio adds depth that a one-page resume cannot.
  • Prepare for interviews: Use your projects as talking points during interviews.

Steps to Build a Strong Portfolio

  • Choose Your Best Work: Select three to six projects that highlight your unique skills and strengths.
  • Write Clear Case Studies: For each project, include your role, the problem, your process, and the outcome.
  • Use Simple Navigation: Make it easy for visitors to find projects and key information.
  • Focus on Accessibility: Add alt text to images, use readable headings, and ensure keyboard navigation.
  • Get Feedback: Share your portfolio with peers or a career coach for constructive feedback.

Portfolio FAQ

  • Do I need a portfolio for every role? Portfolios are essential for UX, design, data, and development roles, and helpful for many others.
  • How long should my portfolio be? Focus on quality over quantity; three to six strong projects is ideal.
  • Should I include personal projects? Yes, personal projects can showcase your passion and initiative.
  • Can I include group projects? Yes, but clearly describe your individual contributions.
  • How often should I update it? Update your portfolio every semester or after major projects.
